## Abstract Requirements

- The Automated Digital Forensics Evidence Collection and Analysis System is required to provide a unified digital platform that supports forensic investigation activities in a structured and legally compliant manner. The system must act as a centralized environment for managing digital evidence throughout its lifecycle, from acquisition to reporting.

- The system shall be capable of handling heterogeneous digital evidence originating from multiple sources while maintaining forensic soundness. It must ensure that evidence remains authentic, traceable, and legally admissible throughout the investigation process.

- The system shall support investigative workflows by reducing manual dependency and enabling consistent forensic procedures. It must assist investigators in deriving meaningful insights from digital evidence while maintaining transparency and accountability in all operations.

- The system shall provide a controlled investigative environment that enforces accountability among users and maintains complete traceability of evidence handling. All evidence-related activities must be recorded in a manner suitable for audits and legal scrutiny.

- The system shall be adaptable to evolving digital forensic needs, allowing incorporation of new analysis approaches, datasets, and investigative methodologies without affecting existing evidence integrity.

- The system shall support informed decision-making by presenting analyzed information in an organized and interpretable manner, aiding investigators and legal authorities in understanding forensic findings.

- Overall, the system shall function as a reliable digital forensic support framework that enhances investigation efficiency, preserves evidence credibility, and aligns with standard forensic and legal practices.
